## How DCCN is licensed

DCCN uses a **dual-license model**. The source code is released under the GNU General Public License v3.0 or later (GPL-3.0-or-later), which means it is free to use in open-source projects that comply with the GPL. Organizations that need to use DCCN in proprietary or closed-source software must obtain a **Commercial Exception License** directly from the authors.

This model is the same approach used by Qt, MySQL, and FOSS projects that need to remain free for the community while enabling sustainable development through commercial licensing revenue.

### Tier 4 — Government, defense, and public sector

> **⚠ Do not self-serve this tier. Contact us for a quote before committing to any price.**

Government and public-sector use carries requirements and value that are not captured by the standard commercial tiers. Published list prices ($1,200 or $6,000/yr) are calibrated for private-sector organizations and will systematically under-price government deployments if applied directly.

**Why government pricing is negotiated separately:**

- **Procurement vehicle requirements.** Federal, state, and local agencies frequently require specific contract vehicles (GSA Schedule, BPA, IDIQ, sole-source justification) that carry their own administrative cost and terms. Pricing must reflect this overhead.
- **Compliance obligations.** Deployments in regulated federal environments may require FISMA/FedRAMP alignment, FIPS 140-2 validated cryptographic modules, NIST SP 800-53 controls documentation, or ITAR considerations for defense-adjacent use. These are scoped and priced per engagement.
- **Multi-year budget cycles.** Government contracts are often structured as multi-year agreements (2–5 years), base year plus option years, or OTAs. Annual list prices do not map cleanly to these structures.
- **Budget authority.** Agency and department budgets for critical infrastructure software are substantially larger than those at comparable private-sector organizations. A tool like DCCN — providing deterministic, auditable, cryptographically-verifiable state management — has direct applications in audit systems, records management, supply chain integrity, and intelligence data pipelines. It should be priced to reflect that value, not the startup-tier list price.
- **Support and delivery requirements.** Government engagements often require on-site or FedCloud deployment assistance, cleared personnel, or coordination with a prime contractor or systems integrator.

**Signals that government custom pricing applies to you:**

- Your organization is a federal civilian agency, DoD component, intelligence community element, or a contractor/subcontractor using DCCN on a government program of record
- Your organization is a state or local government agency deploying DCCN in a system of record or public-facing service
- You are a research institution operating under a federal grant where the software will be used on government-funded work
- Your procurement requires a W-9, System for Award Management (SAM.gov) registration, or a signed Teaming Agreement
- The deployment will be on GovCloud, IL2/IL4/IL5, or a classified network enclave

**Starting point for budget conversations:**

While every engagement is different, government and public-sector pricing typically starts at **$18,000/year** for a single-program license and scales based on agency size, deployment scope, support requirements, and contract vehicle. Multi-year agreements are available and encouraged.

## What the Commercial Exception License grants

A Commercial Exception License grants you:

- The right to use DCCN in proprietary, closed-source software without triggering GPL copyleft obligations
- The right to distribute applications incorporating DCCN without releasing your application's source code
- The right to modify DCCN for internal use (modifications remain subject to the license terms)
- A non-exclusive, non-transferable, worldwide license for the term of the agreement

It does **not** grant:

- The right to sublicense DCCN to third parties as a standalone product
- The right to relicense DCCN itself under a different license
- Ownership of or intellectual property rights in DCCN

Full license agreement text is provided upon purchase.

## Frequently asked questions

**I'm a contractor building a system for a government agency. Which tier applies?**
Contact us (Tier 4). The relevant question is not who signs the check — it is whether the end deployment is on a government program of record. Contractors working on government programs should use Tier 4 pricing and may need to pass costs through to the government as a direct cost on the contract.

**We're a startup. Do we need a commercial license on day one?**
If your product is closed-source, yes — even in pre-revenue stage. If you are genuinely open-source and GPL-compatible, you never need a commercial license. If you are uncertain, email us and we will help you assess.

**Can I evaluate DCCN before purchasing?**
Yes. You may use DCCN under the GPL for internal evaluation for up to 60 days before deciding to pursue a commercial license. Evaluation use must not involve distribution of a product or deployment in production.

**Is pricing negotiable?**
Standard commercial and enterprise tiers are fixed. Academic pricing has limited flexibility. Government and public-sector pricing is always negotiated — that is the point of Tier 4.

**Do you offer volume discounts for multiple programs within one agency?**
Yes. Enterprise-wide agency agreements covering multiple programs, offices, or divisions are available and are structured differently from per-program licenses. Raise this in your initial contact.
